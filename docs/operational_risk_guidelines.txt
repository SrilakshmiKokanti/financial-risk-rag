OPERATIONAL RISK MANAGEMENT GUIDELINES - Version 1.8 | June 2024

1. DEFINITION
Risk of loss from failed internal processes, people, systems, or external events.
Includes legal risk; excludes strategic and reputational risk.

2. RISK CATEGORIES
Internal Fraud: unauthorized activity, theft.
External Fraud: hacking, phishing, third-party theft.
Employment: discrimination, health and safety violations.
Business Disruption: system failures, utility outages.
Execution: transaction errors, data entry errors.

3. RISK ASSESSMENT
RCSA: annually by each business unit.
KRIs: monitored monthly; breaches trigger immediate review.
Losses above $10,000 recorded within 48 hours.

4. CONTROLS
Preventive: segregation of duties, dual auth for payments above $100k,
mandatory vacation (10+ days), background checks.
Detective: transaction monitoring, audit trails, reconciliation.
Corrective: incident response, BCP, insurance.

5. BUSINESS CONTINUITY
BCP tested semi-annually.
RTO: 4 hours for critical systems. RPO: 1 hour max data loss.

6. TECHNOLOGY RISK
Critical patches: within 72 hours.
Pen testing: annually and after major changes.

7. REGULATORY CAPITAL
Standardized Approach (SA) under Basel III.
Capital maintained at 115% of regulatory minimum.

8. TRAINING
All staff: annual operational risk training.
High-risk roles (IT, Finance, Compliance): semi-annual.
